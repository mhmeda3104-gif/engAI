Voltage & Ohm's Law

Virtual Lab Objectives:

Understand the operation of measuring instruments as presented by Dr. Zahraa Hashem.

1. Electric Current (I)

The rate of flow of electric charge through a point in the circuit. Measured in Amperes (A).

2. Electric Voltage (V)

The potential difference between two points; the force that drives electrons. Measured in Volts (V).

Engineering Note:

"Current is directly proportional to voltage when resistance is constant ($I \propto V$)."

Laboratory Connection Protocols

Voltmeter

Must be connected in PARALLEL

Ammeter

Must be connected in SERIES

Current Measurement

Device Definition:

An instrument used to measure the intensity of the electric current ($I$) flowing in a closed circuit.

Connection Method

Always connect in SERIES with the load.

PRO-TIP / WARNING:

Never connect an ammeter in parallel with the source; its very low internal resistance will cause a Short Circuit!

Ohm's Relationship

Current is inversely proportional to Resistance ($I \propto 1/R$). High resistance results in lower current readings.

Digital Multimeter: Measuring Resistance, Voltage, and Current

Multimeter & Resistance

Why use a Multimeter?

It allows AI Engineers to verify resistor values and check for continuity in complex circuit boards.

Ohm ($\Omega$) Measurement Setup

1. Turn the dial to the Ohm symbol (**$\Omega$**).
2. Connect Red lead to ($V\Omega$) and Black to (COM).
3. Touch the probes to both ends of the resistor.

Golden Rule

Resistance is a physical property of the material and depends on:

  • Resistivity (Material type).
  • Length of the conductor.
  • Cross-sectional area.

"Resistance acts as the guardian, preventing current surges that could damage sensors."
